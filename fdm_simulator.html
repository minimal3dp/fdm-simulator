FEA Model Notes

This model predicts the anisotropic material properties based on your filament's datasheet values and slicer settings.

  • The predicted values are for the **part as printed**, not the raw filament.
  • **Higher 'Bonding Perfection'** (better layer adhesion) dramatically increases vertical strength.
  • The **Infill Pattern** (Rectilinear vs. Concentric) has a major impact on how the part behaves under load from different directions.
  • Use these properties in FEA software to simulate real-world performance of your specific part geometry.

Data Sources & References

This simulator is built on machine learning models trained using data from the following academic papers and datasets. The recommendations are also derived from the findings within this research.

  1. Kaggle Model: Fumetto, A. (2022). 3D Printer Dataset. Kaggle. Retrieved from kaggle.com/datasets/afumetto/3dprinter
    (Source for data.csv)
  2. C3 Model: Wang, L., Jiang, J., Dong, Y., Ghita, O., Zhu, Y., & Sucala, V. I. (2024). Machine learning enabled 3D printing parameter settings for desired mechanical properties. Virtual and Physical Prototyping, 19(1).
    (Source for C3-RAW DATA.csv)
  3. FEA Model: Lee, J., & Tucker, C. (2025). Data-Driven 3D-Printed Material Data Prediction From Benchmark Specimens. ASME Journal of Computing and Information Science in Engineering, 25(11).
    (Source for 3D_Printing_Data.xlsx - Sheet1.csv)
  4. Fatigue Model: Azadi, M., & Dadashi, A. (2022). Experimental fatigue dataset for additive-manufactured 3D-printed Polylactic acid biomaterials under fully-reversed rotating-bending loadings. Data in Brief, 41.
    (Source for 1-s2.0-S2352340922000580-mmc1.xlsx - Data.csv)
  5. General ML Methodology: Panico, A., Corvi, A., Collini, L., & Sciancalepore, C. (2025). Multi objective optimization of FDM 3D printing parameters set via design of experiments and machine learning algorithms. Scientific Reports, 15.
  6. General ML Methodology: Jatti, V.S., Sapre, M.S., Jatti, A.V., Khedkar, N.K., & Jatti, V.S. (2022). Mechanical Properties of 3D-Printed Components Using Fused Deposition Modeling. Applied System Innovation, 5(112).
  7. Digital Twin Concept: Rojek, I., Marciniak, T., & Mikołajewski, D. (2024). Digital Twins in 3D Printing Processes Using Artificial Intelligence. Electronics, 13(3550).
