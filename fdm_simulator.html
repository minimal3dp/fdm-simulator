FEA Model Notes

This model predicts the anisotropic material properties based on your filament's datasheet values and slicer settings.

  • The predicted values are for the **part as printed**, not the raw filament.
  • **Higher 'Bonding Perfection'** (better layer adhesion) dramatically increases vertical strength.
  • The **Infill Pattern** (Rectilinear vs. Concentric) has a major impact on how the part behaves under load from different directions.
  • Use these properties in FEA software to simulate real-world performance of your specific part geometry.

Data Sources & References

This tool is an educational simulator based on machine learning models trained on published research data. Always perform physical tests for critical applications.

  1. Kaggle Model: Based on the "3D Printer" dataset by Afumento. [Source Link]
  2. C3 Model: Based on the dataset from "Machine learning enabled 3D printing parameter settings for desired mechanical properties" (Wang et al., 2024). [Paper Link]
  3. FEA Model: Based on the dataset from "Data-Driven 3D-Printed Material Data Prediction From Benchmark Specimens" (Lee & Tucker, 2025). [Paper Link]
  4. Fatigue Model: Based on the dataset from "Experimental fatigue dataset for additive-manufactured 3D-printed Polylactic acid biomaterials" (Azadi & Dadashi, 2022). [Paper Link]
  5. Material Properties: Initial values adapted from "Simplify3D - Materials Guide Properties Table". [Source Link]
